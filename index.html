Chinese zodiac is called 生肖 or Shēngxiào in mandarin. It is a repeating cycle of 12 years with each year being represented by an animal. Traditionally, these animals were used to date the year. This year, 2017, is the year of the Rooster. The Chinese Zodiac, like our zodiac is also used for fortune-telling, and also to determine your "personality". One such form of fortune-telling is love compatibility between you and your partner. Additionally, it is believed to be bad luck when your "year" comes around. According to legend, during that year, your animal will offend the god of age, causing you to have bad luck for the rest of the year.

Differences between the Western Zodiac and the Chinese Zodiac

The Chinese 12-part cycle corresponds to years, rather than months. The Chinese zodiac is represented by 12 animals, whereas some of the signs in the Western zodiac are not animals, despite the implication of the Greek etymology of "zodiac".The animals of the Chinese zodiac are not associated with constellations spanned by the ecliptic plane.
